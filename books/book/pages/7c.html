But fame was not something Florence wanted: She was more interested in helping others than becoming famous. She even changed her name to "Miss Smith" when she travelled back home on the train so no one would recognise her. It must of been hard for her to keep a low profile, as I would often read about her in the newspapers.

She travelled to other military hospitals and tried to improve them. She met with Queen Victoria and told her what was wrong with Army hospitals. Her feedback changed the training of the Army doctors, made hospitals cleaner and soldiers were being given better food and clothing.

She was given a large funding award from the British Government to help set up more nurse training colleges. She used the money to create St. Thomas's Hospital and the Nightingale Training School for Nurses where one of her most famous books: 'Notes for Nursing' was used to train future nurses.
