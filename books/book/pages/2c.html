She was in charge of about 40 volunteers who she had trained as nurses and another 15 nuns who had all come from Britain to help in the hospital a few weeks ago.

As she placed her lamp on the floor next to my bed, she whispered: "Hello Major, how are you feeling tonight?"

I spoke in between gasps of air whilst clenching my teeth: "Hello Ma'am. I am still in as much pain here and here", [pointing to my leg and chest] "as last night. I can not sleep because of it... Is there anything you can do to help me?"

She placed her hand on her chin, taking a moment to look at the broken man in front of her and then took the notes beside my bed up and looked through them.

"I'm sorry you are suffering. You know the doctors are doing everything they can for you and the others. They are low on supplies and medicine. They themselves hardly sleep and are overworked. With all the injured the war produces, they are finding it hard to help you all."
