War Is Over! War Is Over!" The headlines of the papers screamed.

"At last", I thought to myself. I had only spent weeks in battle but then long months being treated for my injuries. I saw the horrors of war and the barracks turn from a place of hell into a modern, hygienic hospital and now, finally, I was back home in England. There was cheers for us when our ship arrived at the docks but it was nothing like the response that Florence received.

When the conflict ended, a year and half after she first sailed out, she returned and was met with a hero's welcome. After a portrait of Florence carrying her lamp and tending to patients appeared in the press, she had become a real hero to lots of people. It was this image that was being printed on bags, mats and other souvenir items. Unknown to her, whilst she was away, her family home was receiving lots of fan mail, poems and requests for marriage. Florence was famous.
