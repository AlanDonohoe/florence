After a few weeks, I was moved into another ward in the barracks hospital: my condition was improving and I had to give my bed up to someone worse off than me. I spent a long time here, waiting for my body to repair. The hospital was dirty and overcrowded. I entered the war thinking that I would die by being shot and I was now scared of dying of typhus, typhoid, cholera or dysentery. You were ten times more likely to die of a disease caught in the hospital than you were from being killed in battle. I had watched a lot of men die from disease around me... Still, my pain subsided and I could at least sleep at night now. I wasn't sent back to the battlefields as my wounds got infected: god, they itched like hell.

We were all so far away from home; it was lonely out in here in Crimea, even with the other British soldiers to talk to. We were forced to wait, impatiently daydreaming of home, for any letters or news.
