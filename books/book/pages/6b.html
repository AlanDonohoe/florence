Inside the barracks hospital there were no proper toilets and the floor was covered in human waste. They was not enough beds so men lay on stretchers and on the floor, unwashed, in their own waste. The building was over a large cesspit and now the drains were blocked, rats and insects were everywhere and the smell was terrible. The troops were used to having to eat mouldy bread and old, tough meat and live in disgusting conditions. There was no one who could fight to change things. Until Florence.

Without: decent food; fresh water; clean clothes, bandages and a hygienic environment, Florence knew that the soldiers were going to die from all the disease creeping through the wards. There was only one thing for it: Florence and her team started cleaning and making sure the soldiers were properly fed and clothed. She would do whatever it took to stop the senseless suffering and death.
