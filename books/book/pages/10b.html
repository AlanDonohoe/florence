I was not the only one hurt due to the Crimean War. It looks like Florence caught 'Crimean Fever' whilst she was looking at the soldiers in the Scutari barracks. By the time she turned 38 she was bedridden; staying in her property in Mayfair, London. But that didn't stop her campaigning.

She poured over statistical data from her bed and wrote letters to newspapers and politicians, fighting to improve hospitals and society.

As her health deteriorated she had to pay for private nurses to come and tend to her. She realised that most people were not as rich as her and so would not have such a luxury. To help those that had to look after themselves or their own family, her book: "Notes on Nursing", helped educate people about how to care for sick relatives. She also campaigned to improve the sanitation for poorer neighbours up and down the country. She sent trained nurses into the factories where poorer people worked to help treat those who couldn't afford healthcare. This vision of providing healthcare available to everyone, regardless of their case or income, helped inspire the National Health Service that was to be born years later.
