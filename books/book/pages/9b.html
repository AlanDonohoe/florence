By now (1880) scientists had proved that diseases were spread by 'germs' (bacteria and viruses) which supported Florence's idea that cleanliness should be a top priority in hospitals and in general life.

Florence had been involved in improving the health of the British army in India since her experiences in the Crimean War. She received reports from India that convinced her again that it was lack of sanitary conditions that was causing unnecessary deaths in India. She then campaigned for assistance for victims of famine and to improve sanitary conditions. She became an authority on public sanitation for both the Indian military and civilians, despite actually never going to India herself.
