After pouring over the data on patient death rates and other measurements that Florence had recorded at Scutari, she realised there was another way to convince others to help her improve hospitals and save lives.

When she met Queen Victoria, she convinced the Queen to set up a Royal Commission into the health of the army and she persuaded leading mathematicians and statisticians to help her analyse the large amount of data that she and others had collected on the health of the army.

It was through analysing this data that they discovered a shocking truth: 16,000 of the 18,000 deaths in the Crimean War were not due to battle wounds but to preventable diseases, spread by poor hygiene and sanitation.
