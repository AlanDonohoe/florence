Due to her demands, a team came over from Britain and flushed out the broken sewers and fixed the ventilation so that the smell of the toilets was gone and there was fresh air in the wards instead of coughs and sneezes from injured soldiers. That sorted out some of the men. They looked healthier already.

Florence's stubbornness also meant we had beds, rather than left on stretchers on the ground, with clean white sheets, rather than dirt and blood-stained rags. She and her nurses cleaned the whole hospital, top to bottom. There was hardly any flies, or maggots and no one had seen a rat or a mouse in long time. Every now and again I would still make fun out of some of the younger soldiers…

“Oi! James! Look out! There’s rat by your bed!”
