her family (her father: William, her mother: Frances and her elder sister, also called Frances (her nick name was ‘Pop’) moved back to England. They were a wealthy family: William was a rich banker and had inherited a large amount of land and property. They owned a number of large houses where they could live. In the summer they lived in Derbyshire and in winter they lived in Hampshire and they were rich enough to afford servants. She was good at school and enjoyed mathematics the most.

I thought to myself that this all sounded like the makings of a very happy, content, privileged upbringing but as Florence began to make clear, this world she was born into and the expectations of her parents and society on her, as young upper-class woman was not what she wanted.

She explained that she was expected to find and marry a rich man, raise children and look after their home but she wanted so much more than that. By the time she was sixteen she knew she had something more important in life to do: she wanted to help others. Her grandfather was an anti-slavery campaigner and she had a strong sense of right and wrong running in her family… she was not scared to fight for change.
